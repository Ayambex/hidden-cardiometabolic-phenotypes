# Research Protocol

## Project Title

Identification of Hidden Cardiometabolic Phenotypes Associated With Early Heart Failure Risk in U.S. Adults Using Explainable Machine Learning

## Background

Heart failure with preserved ejection fraction (HFpEF) is an increasingly prevalent form of heart failure associated with obesity, hypertension, diabetes, metabolic dysfunction, and chronic inflammation. These risk factors often develop gradually and may coexist in complex patterns before overt heart failure becomes clinically apparent.

Traditional risk assessment methods typically evaluate individual risk factors independently and may fail to identify early cardiometabolic deterioration occurring across multiple physiological systems.

Machine learning and phenotyping approaches provide an opportunity to uncover hidden subgroups of individuals who share similar cardiometabolic profiles and may represent early pathways toward heart failure.

## Research Question

Can machine learning identify hidden cardiometabolic phenotypes associated with early heart failure risk in U.S. adults?

## Objectives

### Primary Objective

Identify latent cardiometabolic phenotypes in U.S. adults using unsupervised machine learning techniques.

### Secondary Objectives

1. Characterize the clinical, demographic, and lifestyle features of each phenotype.
2. Identify phenotypes exhibiting patterns consistent with early cardiometabolic deterioration.
3. Explore the implications of these phenotypes for early risk identification and precision medicine.

## Dataset

National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey (NHANES)

## Planned Variables

* Age
* Sex
* Race/Ethnicity
* BMI
* Waist Circumference
* Blood Pressure
* HbA1c
* Fasting Glucose
* Lipids
* Inflammatory Markers
* Sleep Variables
* Lifestyle Factors

## Methodology

1. Data engineering and preprocessing
2. Feature engineering
3. Dimensionality reduction
4. Clustering analysis
5. Network analysis
6. Explainable AI and phenotype interpretation

## Expected Outcome

Identification of clinically meaningful cardiometabolic phenotypes that may reflect early deterioration pathways associated with future heart failure risk.
