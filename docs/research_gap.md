# Research Gap Analysis

## Working Research Question
Can explainable machine learning identify hidden cardiometabolic phenotypes associated with early heart failure risk in U.S. adults?

## What Prior Studies Have Done
Prior studies have used clustering and machine learning to identify phenotypes among patients who already have heart failure, including HFpEF. Other studies have examined cardiometabolic risk factor clustering in adult populations.

## Identified Gap
Most existing work focuses on individuals with diagnosed heart failure or on broad cardiometabolic risk clustering. While prior studies have successfully identified phenotypes among patients with established HFpEF, less is known about whether hidden cardiometabolic phenotypes can be detected in the general population before overt heart failure develops. Identifying these early phenotypes may improve risk stratification and support earlier preventive interventions.

## Proposed Contribution
This study will use NHANES data and unsupervised machine learning to identify hidden cardiometabolic phenotypes in U.S. adults and determine whether these phenotypes exhibit characteristics associated with early pathways toward HFpEF.
The identification of early cardiometabolic phenotypes may provide insights into preclinical pathways leading to HFpEF and inform future precision prevention strategies.

## Future Directions
1. Investigate whether phenotypes differ by sex.
2. Develop a model to identify individuals belonging to high-risk phenotypes.
3. Explore whether certain phenotypes may represent early pathways toward HFpEF.
