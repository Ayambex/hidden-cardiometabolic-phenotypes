# Research Gap Analysis

## Working Research Question
Can unsupervised machine learning identify hidden cardiometabolic phenotypes in U.S. adults that may represent early pathways toward Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction (HFpEF)?

## What Prior Studies Have Done
Prior studies have used clustering and machine learning to identify phenotypes among patients with established HFpEF. Other studies have identified cardiometabolic and renal phenotypes in the general population and demonstrated the importance of cardiometabolic risk clustering. However, these studies were not specifically designed to identify early phenotypes that may represent pathways toward future HFpEF.

## Identified Gap
Most existing work focuses on individuals with diagnosed heart failure or on broad cardiometabolic risk clustering. While prior studies have identified phenotypes among patients with established HFpEF and cardiometabolic phenotypes in the general population, less is known about whether sex-specific hidden cardiometabolic phenotypes associated with early pathways toward HFpEF can be detected before overt heart failure develops. Furthermore, the clinical utility of these phenotypes for precision prevention remains uncertain.

## Proposed Contribution
This study will use NHANES data and unsupervised machine learning to identify sex-specific hidden cardiometabolic phenotypes in U.S. adults and determine whether these phenotypes exhibit characteristics associated with early pathways toward HFpEF.
The identification of sex-specific early cardiometabolic phenotypes may provide insights into preclinical pathways leading to HFpEF and inform future precision prevention strategies.

## Future Directions
1. Investigate whether phenotypes differ by sex.
2. Develop a model to identify individuals belonging to high-risk phenotypes.
3. Explore whether certain phenotypes may represent early pathways toward HFpEF.
